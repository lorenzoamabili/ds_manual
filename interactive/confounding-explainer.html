Causal inference · a draggable paradox

One lurking variable can flip the entire story.

Each point is a customer. Colour marks a hidden group — say, a customer cohort that affects both how much they were exposed to a treatment and their outcome. Inside every group the effect is positive. Drag the confounder up and watch the naive line that ignores the group swing negative. That reversal is exactly why Project 3's naive estimate reads −1.5 when the truth is +3.

The "adjustment" methods in Project 3 — regression control, IPW, matching, doubly-robust — are all formal ways of looking within the groups instead of pooling across them. Set reveal groups off to see what a model that never measured the confounder sees: a single misleading trend. The lesson of doc 09 in one slider — the estimate you trust depends entirely on what you condition on.
